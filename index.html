Our Mission:

Given the complex and difficult to navigate nature of the American immigration system, this project attempts to provide migrants, their family members and other relevant parties a resource that will aid them in identifying circumstances under which they may be asked, or required, to submit to DNA collection.


This resource contains a number of “wayfinders” - graphical illustrations of the pathways through which migrants interact with agencies of the United States government. Relevant agencies include the United States Citizenship and Immigration Services (“USCIS”), Customs and Border Patrol (“CBP”) and Immigration and Customs Enforcement (“ICE”).


Each wayfinder is intended to not only assist the interested parties in understanding their complex legal system surrounding the collection of DNA from migrants, but also to provide resources for ensuring that the data they provide is retained in a manner that is safe and secure and not used in a way that can harm them or their family members.
